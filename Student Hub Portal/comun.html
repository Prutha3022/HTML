Know more about Holidays and Events

The 2026 calendar for Ahmedabad features a diverse blend of national public holidays and regional cultural festivals. Key dates include Republic Day on Jan 26, Holi on March 4, Independence Day on Aug 15, and Diwali on Nov 8. The year also incorporates major seasonal and religious events like Maha Shivaratri, Raksha Bandhan, and Christmas.

Jan 01 – New Year's Day: Celebrated globally to mark the beginning of the Gregorian calendar year with parties, gatherings, and resolutions. Jan 14 – Uttarayan (Makar Sankranti/Pongal): A major harvest festival widely celebrated in Gujarat with colorful kite-flying, community feasts, and vibrant fairs.Jan 26 – Republic Day: A national public holiday commemorating the date the Constitution of India came into effect in 1950, celebrated with parades and patriotic events.

Feb 05 – Maha Shivaratri: A major Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Shiva. Observances include night-long vigils, fasting, and visits to temples.Feb 14 – Valentine's Day: A special observance celebrating romance and affection, marked with gifts, dates, and expressions of love.

Mar 04 – Holi: The vibrant spring festival of colors. People gather to throw colored powders (gulal), dance, and enjoy traditional sweets like gujiya.Mar 21 – Eid al-Fitr (Ramadan Id): An important Islamic festival marking the end of the fasting month of Ramadan, celebrated with communal prayers and festive meals.Mar 27 – Good Friday: A solemn Christian holiday commemorating the crucifixion of Jesus Christ, observed with prayers and church services.

Apr 02 – Ram Navami: Celebrates the birth of Lord Rama, marked by temple visits, readings of the Ramayana, and the organization of grand processions.Apr 06 – Mahavir Jayanti: A significant Jain festival celebrating the birth of Lord Mahavira, marked by prayers, processions, and acts of charity.

May 01 – Labour Day / Gujarat Day: Celebrates the foundation of the state of Gujarat in 1960 alongside international workers' rights.May 10 – Mother's Day: A day of observance to honor mothers and motherhood, typically celebrated with family meals and thoughtful gifts.May 25 – Buddha Purnima: Commemorates the birth, enlightenment, and passing of Gautama Buddha.

June 21 – Father's Day: A day to honor and celebrate fathers, typically spent with family and casual gatherings.

July 06 – Muharram (Ashura): An Islamic observance marking the start of the lunar Hijri New Year, often a time of remembrance and reflection.
